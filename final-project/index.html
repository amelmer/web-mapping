Food Access in San Jose


Measure Access

There are many ways to assess food access in an area. This map allows you to assess access using two measures, either separately or in combination.
Calculating a travel time range will outline on the map what is available within your specified range. Setting a time of day will filter the food options to what is open at the selected time.

Tips for Using the Map

  1. Only select categories in the top right box that are useful for your assessment of access!
  2. The recommended order for interacting with the map is as follows:
    • Neighborhood Boundaries
    • Bike Lanes
    • Bike Racks and Affordable Housing
    • Travel Time Measure (and zoom in on outlined area)
    • Food Layers (Grocery Stores, Restaurants, etc)
    • Time of Day Measure
  3. When setting a time of day, some locations may disappear because their hours of operation are not known and not because they are closed.
  4. Selecting a specific day of the week and setting time to "any" will filter the data to options that are open at any time on that day
    • Setting day to "any" and selecting a specific time will not filter the options.
  5. Clicking on the dot of a displayed food option will show the business' name!

Food Categories

  • Charitable Food: food pantries; generally places to get free food
  • Convenience Stores: stores offering a limited range of groceries
  • Drinks and Sweets: beverage or sweets focused shops, including cafes, bakeries, and ice cream shops
  • Grocery Stores: stores offering full range of groceries, including fresh produce
  • Specialty Grocers and Farms: farm stands and specialty grocers, such as butchers and fishmongers
  • Restaurants: full service restaurants and fast food
  • If a dot is outlined in dark green, that indicates that the business accepts SNAP.
